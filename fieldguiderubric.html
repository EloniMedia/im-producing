Field Guides
Field Guides are intended to be a chance for you to explore in-depth a concept or a trend in interactive media and web development that is currently shaping the field and will continue to do so in the next five years. You will be asked to present for 8 to 10 minutes on a topic of your choice from the list of suggested topics.
The intent is to provide an overview of the technology itself, a suggestion as to where to begin in terms of learning, developing, and devising strategy for use of the technology, and code samples and links to resources should we want to get started with any of these.

Criteria:

  • Completeness of topic coverage
  • Code and/or functional samples
  • Responded to all prompts in an appropriate manner
  • Provided links to relevant information sources
  • Compiled resources into a blog post (public)
  • Presentation: informal, informational
  • Presentation: thorough within limits of time
  • Presentation: opens up topics to invite exploration outside of class
  • Presentation: 10 minutes +/- 1 minute
  • Presentation: audience engagement

List of questions to address

  1. What is it?
  2. Who invented it (or how did it/they emerge)?
  3. Provide a basic timeline or history of the technology.
  4. What is the technology intended to do?
  5. How is it currently being utilized?
  6. What is the future of this technology?
  7. What other technologies are similar to this technology (or competitors within this topic area)?
  8. Provide five resources that would serve as a good starting point for someone to learn more about the topic.
  9. Provide three code samples either as Gists or a Github repo, a set of Codepen posts, or similar.
  10. Cite all sources of your code, your research, etc. in your blog post through links or endnotes.
