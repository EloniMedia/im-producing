Course Description

This course covers the fundamental practices associated with interactive media production, including interface design, applied multimedia and usability refinement. In the effort to provide users with optimized opportunities for choice and control, students will apply design guidelines such as Shneiderman's eight Golden Rules and production/design trends emerging in various industries. Students will author interactive experiences and explore historical origins as well as today's best practices.

Goal

Understand fundamental interactive media development concepts using common industry development tools in order to reinforce technique that may be applied beyond the scope of this course. Emerge with enhanced technical skills and theoretically-informed insights into interactivity and its increasingly significant role in a variety of communications contexts.

Summary of Assignments

This course is centered around the theme of interactive media in context. Given that media has become more immersive and prevalent and we interact with digital media through all sorts of devices on a daily basis, the charge I am presenting this class is to consider what interactive media looks like in a particular context. Initially, I had considered centering this course around the theme of "education in context," but the topic of education seemed to be too limiting.

I am asking you all to think about how your daily lives (or the lives of members of your communities) can be made better through interactive media, whether that interaction occurs at a desk, on a laptop computer, on a tablet or a smart phone, on hardware designed for a particular purpose, in dashboards on cars, on treadmills at the gym, or wherever you see a need for a digital device and interface to improve a process.

I ask that you think about how your work in the Interactive Media MA program is informed by your past experiences, your career goals, and how you wish to position yourself in this expansive and exciting field.
